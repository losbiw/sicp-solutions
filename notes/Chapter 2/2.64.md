a) left-result recursively creates the left branch of the new tree, where the elements included in the left tree are the elements left of the (roughly) middle element.
The returned result ("left-result") includes sorted left tree, as well as all other non-left elements (i.e middle element + soon-to-be right hand side of the tree).
Similarly, right hand side of the final tree is obtained through recursively turning the non-left-elements, omitting the middle element, into a tree. The remainder of this operation are all the elements that were not included in the final tree at all. 
The results are also joined into a tree with middle element as the head, and the corresponding left and right branches, as well as the remaining elements from the right-hand side of the initial list that were not included.
