## Serialized exchange

One exchange can occur per a pair of accounts, i.e if account 1 and account 2 are being exchanged, the exchange between accounts 2 and 3 will be delayed. Thus, if operations are done one at a time, it is impossible to violate the condition that the initial amounts will be preserved, albeit in a different order (an exchange between 20 and 10 will always yield 20 and 10 or 10 and 20)

Since exchanges are no longer serialized, an exchange between accounts 1 & 2 could be happening at the same time as an exchange between accounts 1 & 3. Thus, they may calculate the difference between accounts based on the old amounts. However, since deposits and withdrawals for individual accounts are still serialized, the deposit and withdrawal procedures themselves always have access to the latest value of the amount for their account, thus preserving the overall sum of balances.
