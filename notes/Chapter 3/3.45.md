In Louis's version, when the `serialized-exchange` procedure is called, each action ends up being serialized multiple times. An exchange is itself serialized with both serializers like so:
`(serializer1 (serializer2 exchange))`

However, the the account1 withdrawal ends up being serialized with serializer1, as well as the account2 deposit being serialized with serializer2 due to Louis's serialization of the individual procedures.

Thus, the exchange procedure is added to the queues for both serializer1 and 2, while relying on deposit and withdrawal procedures which are also reliant on the same serializers, thus never executing (i.e causing a deadlock).
