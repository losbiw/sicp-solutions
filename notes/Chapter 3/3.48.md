Tracing through the original example, if two simultaneous exchanges occur such that: P1: a1->a2 and P2: a2->a1, where P1 acquires a lock on a1's mutex just before P2 acquires a lock on a2's mutex, the exchange ends up in a deadlock.

With the modified exchang where each account has a unique ID (important that they are unique, otherwise the deadlock could still occur), the setup is as such:
- Account 1 ID: 1
- Account 2 ID: 2

Each exchange procedure attempts to first acquire a lock on the lowest numbered account, thus whether the exchange is going from a1 to a2 or the other way around, the first lock to be acquired will always be on a1. Thus, once Peter has entered the protected procedure for a1, Paul will have to wait until Peter has released locks for both a1 and a2.
